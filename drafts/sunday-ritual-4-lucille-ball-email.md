# Sunday Ritual #4 — Lucille Ball
## Email Draft

**Subject:** The Sunday Ritual #4: Lucille Ball and the Table That Held Everything Together

**Preview:** The card table on I Love Lucy wasn't a prop. It was the whole point.

**Body:**

If you grew up watching I Love Lucy, you probably remember the card table.

It showed up constantly. Lucy and Ethel playing canasta across the hall with the Mertzes. Setting up for bridge that somehow never happened because an argument broke out first. Ricky asking, "How about a game of cards?" like it was the most natural thing in the world.

Because it was.

Card games appeared in over 30 episodes. Canasta, bridge, gin rummy, poker. The game changed, but the table didn't. It was always there, pulling four people into the same room.

Off screen, Lucille Ball was the same way. In the late 1960s she fell in love with backgammon. She had permanent tables built into her home. She carried travel sets on vacation. She hosted private tournaments with friends and family that lasted for hours.

A backgammon club in Beverly Hills even named their annual charity tournament after her. Betty White, one of her closest friends, once said Lucy was "always going to teach me backgammon."

Lucy understood something simple. The game is the excuse. The people are the point. You don't sit down at a table to win. You sit down to be with someone.

That's what a weekly game night does. It gives people a reason to show up. And showing up is where everything good starts.
