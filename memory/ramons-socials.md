## The Kallaway Framework (Source Video)

Kallaway's "Internet Money Machine" 4-step playbook:
1. **Avatar + Offer** (Step 0) — ✅ DONE. Women 50+, Canasta set, course as lead magnet.
2. **Competitor Research** — ❌ MISSING. Find 10-20 accounts in niche (micro to medium, 10K-1M), mine outlier videos for patterns.
3. **Batch Testing** — ❌ MISSING. Batches of 10 videos. Test 3 "stacks" per batch. Hold 4 of 5 elements constant (format, idea, hook, script, visuals), change 1. Look for 10x outliers. Need 3 validated winning stacks. Beginners need 50-100 videos (5-10 batches).
4. **Ramps (ManyChat + Email)** — ✅ MOSTLY DONE. Welcome sequence, Sunday Ritual, Better Hand all built. ManyChat not live yet (coming soon).

### Key Kallaway Principles
- Don't pick pillars in a conference room. Let data tell you.
- Content-audience-product fit is everything. Wrong audience = billions of views, zero sales.
- Volume matters. You can't learn from 2-3 videos/month.
- The 4 blockers: can't spot patterns, can't brute-force production, can't iterate, workload of content + business.

## Key Decisions & Constraints

### Setting
- Dark room, talking-head, sitting down, calm delivery
- Agency specializes in "inspirational" videos
- Humor approach: **deadpan observational** (same setting, content makes people smile from recognition)
- NOT skits, NOT props, NOT set changes

### Authenticity Constraint
- Ramon doesn't have a weekly in-person Canasta night (plays online)
- Sources for "Table" content: Amazon product reviews, online play observations, universal game table truths, "women tell me" framing from customer feedback
- Never fake personal game night stories

### Script Constraints
- Max 60 seconds per video
- 130-150 words target
- No em dashes, no hashtags, no buzzwords
- Calm, reflective tone (but hooks can be confrontational/provocative)
- CTA on select videos only (comment-based preferred)

### Gender Voice Rule (Mirror, Don't Lecture)
Ramon is a man speaking to women 50+. Specificity ("women") builds connection, but delivery matters.
- **Keep "women" when mirroring a shared experience:** "After fifty, many women stop hosting" — empathy, observation
- **Switch to "you/your/the ones who" when it could sound prescriptive:** "The women who wait are the loneliest" → "If you're waiting for the invitation, you'll wait forever"
- **Test:** "I see this happening" = mirror ✅ | "Women need to stop doing X" = lecture ❌
- A man standing *beside* his audience, not above them
- Apply this rule to all future script writing, email copy, and content

## Email vs Video (Separate Topic — To Be Discussed)

### Key Insight (decided March 5)
- **Videos and emails serve different jobs.** Don't change email strategy based on video pillar changes.
- Videos (top of funnel) = hook strangers, make them feel SEEN. Needs scroll-stopping content.
- Sunday Ritual email (mid-funnel) = depth for subscribers who already opted in. Reflective famous women stories work perfectly HERE.
- Famous women content isn't bad. It's just better suited for email than TikTok.
