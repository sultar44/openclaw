Observations from Ramon's actual sent emails vs. drafted versions. Used to improve future drafts.

## 2026-03-03 — The Guardian / Owen Duffy (Outreach Email 1)

**Subject:** "Canasta for your family board games list?"
- Question-format subject line, casual and direct

**Style observations:**
- ~130 words — short, within the 150-word guideline
- Opens with "I came across your piece on..." — specific reference to the article
- No flattery/compliment. Just states the fact, then pivots to the product
- Leads with the social angle (women 50+, accountability, weekly gatherings) rather than product features
- Uses storytelling framing: "once a group commits to a weekly night, nobody can skip without letting the table down"
- Offers complimentary set early (paragraph 2)
- Links to all7s.co product page ✅ (not Amazon)
- Sign-off: "Warmly, Ramon / Founder / All7s Games" with link to all7s.co
- No product image attached (unlike our draft template suggestion)
- No mention of "(Product photo attached.)"
- No em dashes ✅

**Key takeaway:** Ramon's actual pitch is more narrative-driven than template-driven. He doesn't just describe the product — he tells a mini-story about the social dynamic. Future drafts should lean into this style: less "what the product is" and more "what happens when people use it."

**Deviation from template:** Skipped the product image attachment. May prefer cleaner emails without attachments for cold outreach.
