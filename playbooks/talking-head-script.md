# Talking Head Video Script Writer — 1 Minute Format

## Purpose
Take any topic and create a 1-minute talking head video script for All7s Games / Ramon's social content. Target audience: women 50+, Canasta players, game night enthusiasts.

---

## The Formula (3-Step Hook + Body + CTA)

Every script follows this structure. Total word count: **140-160 words** (roughly 1 minute spoken at natural pace).

### Step 1: Hook (3-5 seconds, 20-30 words)

Use the **3-part hook formula**:

1. **Context Lean** (1-2 sentences): State the topic clearly so the right viewer self-selects in. Build common ground by referencing a benefit or pain point.
2. **Scroll Stop Interjection** (1 sentence): Use a contrast word (but, however, yet) to stun them. This is the red light that stops the scroll.
3. **Contrarian Snapback** (1 sentence): Snap them in the opposite direction of the initial lean. The bigger the surprise, the stronger the hook.

**Hook type to use (pick one per video):**

| Type | Template | When to use |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **About Me** | "I [accomplished X outcome] using [Y simple method]" | Sharing a personal result |
| **If I** | "If I wanted to [X outcome], I would [Y method]" | Hypothetical / teaching |
| **To You** | "If you're trying to [X outcome], [Y method] will change everything" | Direct advice |
| **Can You** | "Is it possible to [achieve X] in [Y simple condition]?" | Challenge / curiosity |
| **He/She Just** | "[Person] just [accomplished X] by doing [Y relatable thing]" | Case study / story |

**For All7s specifically, prefer "To You" and "About Me" hooks.** The audience responds to warmth and relatability, not hype.

### Step 2: Body (40-45 seconds, 90-110 words)

Deliver **one core idea** with these storytelling techniques woven in:

- **Term Branding**: Give your concept a memorable name. "I call this the Empty Chair Problem" hits harder than "many women lose their social circles." The name creates instant anticipation.
- **Embedded Truths**: Frame everything as already true. Say "when you try this" not "if you try this." Say "the reason this works" not "this might work because." Kill words: if, maybe, might, could, probably.
- **Thought Narration**: Say what the viewer is thinking. "Now you're probably thinking, 'But I don't know enough people to host a game night.'" This builds trust and makes it feel like a 1-on-1 conversation.
- **Negative Frames**: Flip advice into warnings when it hits harder. "This posting mistake is costing you followers" beats "try posting more consistently." Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain motivation.
- **Loop Openers**: After each point, tease the next. "But here's the thing..." / "And that's not even the best part..." Use contrast words (but, however, actually) to reset attention every 15-20 seconds.
- **Contrast Words**: The engine behind everything. Use "but" to split a sentence: lean them one way, snap them back. "Everyone thinks X, but actually Y."

**Body structure:**
1. State the problem or setup (1-2 sentences)
2. Deliver the insight with a term brand if possible (2-3 sentences)
3. Loop opener → give the practical takeaway (2-3 sentences)

### Step 3: CTA (5-10 seconds, 15-25 words)

Soft and warm. Never salesy. One action only.

Examples:
- "If this hit home, drop a ♠️ in the comments."
- "We talk about this stuff every week. Follow along."
- "Our free Canasta course is a good place to start. Link in bio."
- "Save this for your next game night."

## Visual Hook Notes (for Ramon when filming)

- **Text on screen**: 3-5 bold words that capture the context lean. Viewers read faster than they listen.
- **Motion**: Start with some natural movement (walking in, setting down cards, picking up a game piece). Just enough to catch the eye.
- **Staccato delivery**: Hook sentences should be SHORT. Punchy. Compressed. Expand sentence length as you get into the body.

---

## Writing Rules (Humanizer — apply to ALL scripts)

- Use clear, simple language. Short sentences. Active voice.
- **No em dashes.** Use periods or commas instead.
- No metaphors or cliches.
- No hype adjectives or unnecessary adverbs.
- No wrap-up phrases like "in conclusion."
- Banned words: just, really, very, literally, actually, discover, unlock, delve, embark, groundbreaking, harness, illuminate, unveil.
- Write like a real person talking to a friend. Not like AI.
- Vary sentence length. Some short. Some a bit longer to keep rhythm natural.
- **Compress speed to value**: Frontload the best stuff. Anything after they scroll away is a black screen.

## Example Script

**Topic:** Why game nights die after 50

**Hook:**
Most women over 50 have fewer close friends than they did at 30. That's not opinion. That's data. But here's what nobody talks about. It's not that friendships fade. It's that the structure holding them together disappears.

**Body:**
I call this the Empty Chair Problem. When your kids leave, so do the school events, the birthday parties, the built-in reasons to see people. The chairs around your table go empty. Not because anyone stopped caring. Because nobody set the next date. The fix is embarrassingly simple. Pick one night. Make it the same night every week. Text three people. That's it. You don't need a reason. You need a rhythm.

**CTA:**
If this hit home, follow along. We talk about this every week.

**Word count:** ~140 words

## Magnetic Personal Brand Principles (Kallaway — Added Mar 12, 2026)

These principles apply to ALL scripts. They shape tone, framing, and structure beyond the mechanical formula above.

### Positioning: Territory + Beliefs
- **Territory compression**: Ramon's territory is "social card games for women 50+ fighting isolation." Every script must serve this territory. Don't widen it.
- **Contrarian beliefs**: The strongest scripts challenge assumptions ("You're not an introvert, you're out of practice"). Prioritize scripts with a take most people wouldn't say.
- **Origin story**: Periodically include scripts that explain WHY Ramon owns this territory. His mother rebuilt social structure from zero after immigration. Customer emails confirm the pattern. This is his "territory defense." At least 1 per batch.

### Execution: Confidence + Quality
- **Speak in outcomes, not scenarios**: Say "this will" not "this might." Say "let it be you" not "it might as well be you." Kill hedge words: might, maybe, perhaps, could, possibly, it's fine.
- **Effortless zone**: Dark room, sitting, talking head, calm delivery. This IS Ramon's format. Don't change it.
- **Quality bar**: Each script should be the best 60-second video on its topic. Not just a rep. Best on the internet for this specific angle.

### Psychology: Three Mechanisms (every script should hit at least one)
1. **Change how they think** (reframe): Give the viewer a new lens on a familiar problem while they watch. No action required. They leave thinking differently. Example: "That's not peace. That's drift."
2. **Make them feel heard** (relate): Describe their specific emotional experience so precisely they feel understood. Example: "You draft something. Delete it. Draft again." Don't sanitize the feeling.
3. **Speed to first win** (act): Give one small concrete action the viewer can take immediately. Not a life transformation. One micro-step. Example: "Right now, think of the friend you haven't seen in over a month. She's your first text." Add to 30-40% of scripts, not all.

### What NOT to do
- Don't make it about Ramon first. Make it about the viewer first. They care about their problem. Only after consistent value delivery do they start caring about you.
- Don't post lifestyle/morning routine content before earning authority through value.
- Don't cast a wide net. Speak to one specific person in the territory.
- Don't rely solely on tactics ("do X then Y"). Reframes and emotional recognition stick longer than instructions.
